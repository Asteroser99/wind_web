On this page, you can explore different ways to adjust the coil winding pattern to ensure better continuity of the shell.

Correction Variants

  • Choose the desired Convenience value — this defines the number of wedges in the winding and is a key parameter influencing the coil pattern and the interlacing of layers. For certain Convenience values, multiple correction options are available within specific limits.
  • Enable the Mesh structure option if you want to generate a mesh-style winding pattern.

The system solves an integer Diophantine equation to generate a table of valid correction variants. These variants ensure smooth transitions between layers and overall shell continuity.

Applying Coil Correction

From the table, select a coil correction variant that suits your needs.

Pay attention to the correction percentage of the coil period — the lower it is compared to the ideal geodesic value, the better the continuity of the final shell.

A diagram will display the stacking order of the tape for the selected variant.

Click the Correct coil button to apply the selected period correction.


The original geodesic coil is gradually transformed into a corrected non-geodesic coil, with varying degrees of deviation to ensure optimal shell structure.
